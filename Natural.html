Natural and Human Resources Maps

Slope

A Slope map of the study area was generated using a digital elevation model (DEM) with a resolution of 30 meters. The process was carried out using ArcMap software, specifically the Slope tool within the Surface section of the ArcToolbox. The map was created using degrees. Subsequently, it was converted using the Conversion toolbar and transformed into a raster to feature. Reclassification was also performed, categorizing it into 5 classes.

Aspect

The map illustrates that the slope directions in Beni Suef Governorate generally incline from southeast to northwest. The land rises in the southeastern part of the governorate and gradually descends towards the northwest.

Contour

A contour map of Beni Suef Governorate with an 80-meter contour interval was generated based on a digital elevation model (DEM). The contour map of Beni Suef Governorate reveals diverse topography, with both high and low-lying areas. It is noticeable that contour lines converge in some areas, indicating steep slopes, while in other areas, contour lines are more spaced out, indicating gentle slopes.

Hillshade

Shading maps are employed to enhance the appearance of terrain on a map, making it clearer and aiding in understanding the landforms, such as hills, valleys, slopes, and plains. Light gray areas on the map represent slopes facing the light source, while dark gray areas depict slopes moving away from the light source and consequently being in shadow.

Soil

Beni Suef Governorate consists of five types of soils: Sandy dune soil: Located in the western part of Beni Suef Governorate, this soil surrounds the limestone soil from all sides. Nile alluvial soil: Situated in the central part of Beni Suef Governorate, it includes the Nile River and its adjacent lands. Limestone soil Cracked limestone plateau soil: This soil is the largest in terms of area in Beni Suef Governorate and is entirely located east of the Nile River.

Hydrology

Through the map, it becomes evident that the slope in the eastern desert of Beni Suef Governorate starts from the southeast direction and extends towards the northwest. It begins with the first-order ranks, ending with various ranks, all of which flow into the Nile River, with the largest being the seventh rank, which converges in the middle of the Nile. In the central part of Beni Suef Governorate (Nile alluvial soil), the slope starts from the south to the north, beginning with the first-order ranks and ending with various ranks, with the largest being the sixth rank. In the western desert, the slope starts from multiple directions, beginning with the first-order ranks and ending at the largest ranks, which are the fifth rank.
